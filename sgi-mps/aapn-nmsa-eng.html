13. Environmental Considerations

The Government of Canada is committed to reducing its impact on the environment and satisfying clientorganizations' demand for environmentally sensitive products and packaging. In addition to the direct cost reductions in an optimized imaging environment, the reduction, reuse and recycling of packaging and other materials can significantly cut purchasing, operating and disposal costs. While many environmental programs will be applied as result of the use of the NMSOs for printers and digital copiers (in cases where the supplier will not retain ownership of the imaging fleet) as well as the NMSOs for remanufactured toner cartridges, as part of their consulting and managed services, Contractors will be expected to address such criteria as:

  1. The reduction of the volume of packaging materials used when leased or purchased equipment is delivered, including the use of reusable shipping materials, including reusable plastic, crates, and corrugated boxes, where applicable;
  2. The use of recycled and recyclable materials in the packaging of both imaging devices and their consumables – new or remanufactured, where applicable; and,
  3. The use of remanufactured imaging consumables, where applicable.

Contractors are also expected to address such issues as:

  1. The use of devices permitting reduced power consumption;
  2. Third Party Environmental Performance Labeling;
  3. The impact of imaging devices on indoor air quality; and
  4. Environmentally sensitive disposal at the end of life for both imaging devices and consumables containers, especially where it would not be appropriate to conduct disposal operations through Crown Assets Disposal.

1.2 Consulting Services (CS)

1.2.1 Introduction to consulting service offerings

Consulting Services (CS) are associated with the primary aspects of analysis and concept design solution. Secondary consulting services include advanced business analysis activities such as requirement definition, business case development and creation of Statement of Work (SOW) documents.

CS services may be contracted within three (3) distinct service levels. The services are not sequential and may be contracted individually as required. Client organizations may select a full range of service catalogue offerings or select only services to fit budget considerations or to complement their own internally managed optimization initiatives.

1.2.2 Ownership of equipment
CS services will not include the contractor owning the imaging hardware. The contractor will develop a description for an optimized print environment designed to maximize savings and operational effectiveness, however, in this service offering the client organization will retain all ownership and management responsibilities of relevant hardware.

1.2.3 CS – Service Level 1, Inventory and Basic TCO Determination
Inventory and basic TCO services are intended to establish an overview of the organizational business and technical environments to quickly determine the extent of imaging device inventory and spending estimates.

Key aspects of the inventory and TCO process may include:

  • Development of general business and technical profile in terms of equipment inventory, people and organizational aspects; and
  • Determination of basic total cost of ownership (TCO) summary for the current imaging environment.

The tasks associated with, but not necessarily limited to Inventory and Basic TCO services may include:

  • Review equipment inventory including the number of networked and locally attached copiers, printers, scanners and fax devices with their locations;
  • Determine basic Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) summary as a result of equipment, consumable, service, and rental/lease costs. Summary calculation will be based on accepted industry usage and ownership metrics.
1.2.4 CS – Service Level 2, Concept Design
Concept Design services consist of formal planning and requirements definition aimed at developing a business solution to create a reduced cost-imaging environment. Essential recommendations will be made along with specific targets for areas of improvement to ensure the best possible outcomes of a managed service arrangement.

Developed business solutions will clearly define a number of concept design elements, such as: implementation requirements, MPS deliverables, expected quality of service conditions, accurate costs, and procedures for corrective action if problems occur.

Key aspects of the Concept Design process may include:

  • If not previously obtained, review the existing print environment and compile an existing inventory of the imaging devices with associated direct and indirect costs;
  • Further analysis of imaging environment in key areas as required to establish a baseline profile of the current environment, including items such as: current level of standardization, technology used, device utilization, ratio of users to devices, identifying network/floor plans and greening objectives;
  • Identification of improved approaches and best practices to reduce costs and improved efficiencies;
  • Client organization stakeholders and staff involvement to gain a client centric perspective of the business environment to better understand goals, objectives and requirements;
  • Development of optimization strategy to address: equipment right sizing, use of existing equipment, equipment disposal and other anticipated changes related to device changes required to achieve desired objectives;
  • Cost feasibility analysis of alternative approaches which result in maximum savings and best value;
  • Solid solutions based on business objectives rather than technology features;
  • Formalization of identified business requirements into, SOWs and Service Level Agreements (SLA); and
  • Development of implementation strategy and requirements.

The tasks associated with, but not necessarily limited to Concept Design services may include:

Optimization of hardware

  • Review of imaging environment, Information Technology (IT) aspects and analyze strengths and weaknesses;
  • Review of security aspects associated with the imaging infrastructure;
  • Determination of current level of standardization;
  • Develop hardware optimization model to improve the mix of printing and imaging assets encompassing; standardization to reduce model varieties and personal printers; and lower costs while maintaining/improving functionality and user satisfaction;
  • Identify potential opportunities for reallocation of existing assets to minimize new equipment acquisition;
  • Proposed strategy to migrate print volumes to lower cost devices and improve utilization rates;
  • Development of acceptance testing plan to ensure the delivery of ALL: capabilities, functionality's, configuration, security, documentation, training and related aspects as defined in the MPS contractual agreement; and
  • Software and data format so as to meet the organization's requirements for internal reporting, implementation (minimum requirements will be required for any implementation of software within an organization's infrastructure).

Optimization of imaging environment processes

  • Review in-place purchasing practices related to the imaging environment;
  • Review in-house support mechanisms and third party involvement;
  • Determination of how to integrate best practices into client organization's equipment management activities;
  • Identification of transition requirements and major elements that will affect (MS) Work Plan;
  • Proposed processes for Move-Add-Remove-Change (MARC) (asset management, tagging, installation, roll-out and disposal);
  • Development of recommendations to align services with best practices; and
  • Proposed management reporting requirements defining: allowable data formats, styles, or delivery method (e.g. web based, hard copy, raw data).

Reduction of Ownership costs

  • Calculation of the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) summary as a result of an accurate baseline independent of accepted industry usage and ownership metrics;
  • Development of cost models for alternative ownership/management scenarios and corresponding implementation time frames;
  • Development of an optional all inclusive payment model that would include service delivery, installation, consumables, monitoring, MARC requests; and
  • Identification of possible transition costs including fees for termination of leases and/or service agreements, costs of disposal of surplus equipment, updating of wiring, reconfiguring of workspaces.

Identifiable Targets for Improvement

  • Measurement of cost savings and determination of under performing areas;
  • Review and prepare suggestions for improvement of in-place practices and policies that impact on imaging efficiencies;
  • Assistance in meeting the financial targets set by the client organization;
  • Identification of methods of reducing ownership costs beyond initial optimized state;
  • Regular review of financial and operational information to identify issues, concerns, and areas of user frustration;
  • Control of future equipment purchase or lease to minimize changes to optimized environment;
  • Development of approaches and technologies to reduce paper usage; promotion of the use of recycled paper;
  • Assistance with the development of an environmental policy guide for imaging including default duplex for printing and fax, greener ink content, OEM toner cartridges and remanufactured cartridges;
  • Continual review, improvement and development of services offered and collaboration with IT to maximize the advantages of digitization via the network; and
  • Measurement and improvement of user satisfaction.

1.3 Managed Services (MS)

1.3.1 Introduction to Managed Services
Managed Services focus on the primary aspects of transition strategy development and deployment, fleet management, and manage / improve aspects of an imaging environment within a defined contractual agreement over a specified period.

Managed Services providers will work with client organizations to implement and maintain an MPS model. The degree of service delivery may include either a full service model where the vendor manages the entire internal day-to-day imaging environment (e.g. consumables, equipment maintenance, support, training, equipment ownership, tracking and reporting), or a customized model where only selected MPS options are chosen (e.g. consumables and equipment maintenance). The two contracting options are described in the table below.

Contract type Description
MS – Selected services contractual agreement

The MS selected services option provides client organizations with the opportunity to select individual MS services on an as required basis. In some cases, organizations may require limited assistance with their own internally managed MPS initiative, or, in other cases, organizations may choose to build an MPS solution by selecting services from a number of MS service providers.

Selected services will be defined within customized Statements of Work (SOW) and associated work plans and deliverables in resulting request for proposals.

Anticipated MS services available for selection have been organized into the following three (3) Service Levels.

Service Level 1 - Transition services
Service Level 2 - Fleet management
Service Level 3 - Manage and Improve
MS – Bundled Services contractual agreement The MS Bundled service option includes all three service levels, providing organizations with a full set of imaging environment management services within a defined contractual agreement over a specified period. The bundled services will be deployed and managed throughout the contractual time frame according to a work plan supplied in the MS bid proposal describing all services and deliverables.

1.3.3 MS – Service Level 1, Transition services
Transition services focus on the development and execution of a roll out / deployment schedule to define the steps required to transition the current imaging environment to the optimized state and achieve desired results.

Key aspects of the transition process may include:

  • Project management in terms of work plan task control, cost containment, risk mitigation, change control and milestone management;
  • Scheduled management in terms of project status, milestone briefings and regular updates;
  • Change management plan in terms of business transformation and stakeholder communication;
  • Delivery of all assigned work activities (equipment set-up, training, solution set-up, etc.) within specified time-frame and costs;
  • Acceptance testing of solutions to meet specified capabilities, functionality's, security and configuration requirement; and
  • Implementation based on a consistent approach defined within the MPS framework provided optimization tools.

The tasks associated with transition services may include but are not necessarily limited to:

  • Project Management;
  • Acceptance testing;
  • Compatibility testing;
  • Configuration testing;
  • Validation of claims of product capabilities;
  • Equipment disposal;
  • Equipment delivery;
  • Equipment configuration and network connection;
  • Training;
  • Project set up (1-800 telephone numbers, support mechanisms);
  • Logistics;
  • Configuration of Tracking mechanism;
  • Configuration of Reporting mechanism;
  • Varying levels of "hands on" assistance for managing the transition from the existing imaging environment to the identified optimum one as required in the contract;
  • General assistance during transition period from current to optimized state; and
  • Report generation describing: detailed findings, project task status, and results of acceptance testing and specific equipment and environment settings/configuration.
1.3.4 MS – Service Level 2, Fleet Management services
Fleet management services involve the provision of a number of related services throughout the duration of the MPS contractual agreement, including: provision of responsive equipment service/support, provision of timely supply/consumable replenishment, and imaging device tracking, monitoring and reporting services.

Key aspects of the Fleet Management process may include:

  • Effective and efficient equipment maintenance and support services for in-scope new and existing equipment;
  • Provision of supplies and consumables for in-scope existing or new equipment operation;
  • Day-to-day management of imaging fleet operations;
  • Usage monitoring and performance measurement of important fleet information;
  • Activities aimed at gathering information related to usage, support statistics, inventory, and equipment performance for further analysis in determining overall program effectiveness.

Managed Services will be delivered in accordance to established SOW and SLA requirements defined within each Client organization location and standardized approaches established within the MPS framework.

The tasks associated with Fleet Management services may include but are not necessarily limited to:

  • Ordering and replenishment of supplies and consumables such as: toners, inks, paper and other supplies necessary for existing or new equipment operation;
  • Provision of incident management services to provide client organization with easy access to service and supplies;
  • On site situational response (and preventive maintenance), repair, spares and problem resolution time-frames delivered as defined in SLA/SOW contracts for each location;
  • Provision of on premises site manager;
  • Equipment move, add, remove and change services as required;
  • Greening activities such as: proper waste cartridge disposal, equipment disposal, packaging disposal, and energy consumption aspects;
  • Adherence to ALL security requirements;
  • New copier and printer equipment acquired as part of the MPS contract;
  • Services associated with existing copier and printer equipment as defined by the MPS contract, such as: Removal, disposal, relocate, service, supplies, and support; and
  • Management of security aspects of service delivery;
  • Provision of analysis services to provide client organization with items such as: invoice reconciliation, trend identification, cost determination, technology portfolio review for contracts, leases and rentals.

MS vendors may be required to establish a tracking, monitoring and reporting mechanism that will provide the client project authority with a comprehensive overview of selected key imaging environment indicators.

Tracking, monitoring and reporting capabilities may include, but are not necessarily limited to the following:

  • Detailed tracking and reporting for inventory, usage, and consumables;
  • Financial reporting for ALL fleet ownership costs;
  • Regular quality of service delivery (SLA);
  • Client satisfaction;
  • Identification of issues;
  • Measurement of actual versus plan savings;
  • Determination of factors that determine program effectiveness;
  • Identification of methods to improve program effectiveness (manage and improve);
  • Report on greening;
  • To ensure best results, the activities will be co-ordinated according to a monthly, quarterly and annual master schedule. The master schedule will be defined within the contractual agreement;
  • Regular gathering and compilation of equipment usage and service statistics
  • Analysis of tracking information to identify issues, bottlenecks and opportunities for improvement; and
  • Provide report describing detailed findings, equipment usage, issues and related information.
1.3.5 MS – Service Level 3, Manage and Improve services
Manage and Improve services are associated with continuous improvement aspects of imaging environment management, such as: measurement of key metrics, determination of overall progress towards established MPS objectives, and corrective action to be taken as required throughout the duration of the MPS contractual agreement.

Performance measurement metrics may include, but are not necessarily limited to the following:

Measurement of actual versus plan savings;
  • Reduced spending;
  • SLA and quality of service delivery measurement;
  • Greening initiative;
  • Client satisfaction;
  • Over and under utilized equipment;
  • Suggestions for imaging environment improvements;
  • Equipment usage;
  • Identification of issues and problems;
  • Determination of factors that determine program effectiveness; and
  • Identification of methods to improve program effectiveness (manage and improve).

To ensure best results, the activities will be co-ordinated according to a monthly, quarterly and annual master schedule. The master schedule will be defined within the contractual agreement.

1.3.6 Ownership of equipment

At present there are no restrictions on imaging device ownership aspects within MPS service agreements. Client organizations may choose to retain imaging equipment costs or prefer to offload equipment costs to a vendor as part of an MPS solution. Accordingly, the following equipment ownership approaches have been identified (but not limited to):

Equipment ownership type Description
MPS vendor owned equipment approach Vendor will own all existing and new printing copying, scanning and faxing imaging infrastructure hardware. The MPS vendor will be responsible for all existing and new imaging device acquisition costs. In some cases, a vendor may offer to purchase existing imaging devices from a Client organization.
Client organization owned equipment approach Client organization will own all existing and new printing copying, scanning and faxing infrastructure hardware. The Client organization will be responsible for all existing and new imaging devices acquisition costs.
Mixed equipment ownership approach Existing and new printing copying, scanning and faxing infrastructure hardware ownership will vary between Client organizations and Managed Services Vendor. Within this approach, some imaging devices will be owned by the Client organization while other equipment owned by a vendor.

Each CS and MS contractual engagement will be professionally managed and controlled within a formal statement of work (SOW) with clearly defined activities and specific time frames to achieve desired objectives and deliverables.

Project aspects associated with selected services may include:

  • Scope of work;
  • Quality;
  • Project team / resources;
  • Objectives;
  • Deliverables;
  • Project plan and schedule;
  • Status and progress reporting and monitoring;
  • Change management;
  • Pricing; and
  • Findings and recommendations report

Annex B
Glossary of Terms

Acceptance testing
Formal testing conducted to validate the solution meets a set of established acceptance criteria based on stated capabilities and functionality's.
Business Case
refers to an objective analytical assessment of how to best address a defined business opportunity or problem. The analysis includes an assessment of organizational impacts, risks, feasibility, and costs/benefits of various viable alternatives.
Client Organization
Government departments or government agencies wishing to utilize MPS services.
Concept Design

The Concept design process consists of formal planning and requirements definition aimed at developing a business solution to create a reduced cost imaging environment. Essential recommendations are made along with specific targets for areas of improvement to ensure the best possible outcomes of a managed service arrangement.

The solution will clearly define the: implementation requirements, MPS deliverables, expected quality of service conditions, accurate costs, and procedures for corrective action if problems occur.

Key aspects of the Concept Design process include:

  • Further analysis of imaging environment in key areas as required
  • Identification of improved approaches and best practices to reduce costs and improved efficiencies
  • Development of optimization strategy to address: equipment rightsizing, use of existing equipment, equipment disposal and other anticipated changes related to device changes required to achieve desired objectives
  • Cost feasibility analysis of alternative approaches which result in maximum savings and best value
  • Solid solutions based on business objectives rather than technology features
  • Development of implementation strategy and requirements
Consumables
Goods used in the course of operating or using imaging devices, including, but not limited to, such items as: paper, ink, toner, staples, transfer assemblies, drums, fuser components, fuser assemblies, imaging kits, waste containers, user replaceable parts, and other similar items.
Equipment Ownership

The imaging equipment aspects such as: purchasing, lease, rental, title to equipment and disposal. Within MPS the following three (3) equipment ownership models are recognized:

MPS vendor owned model
defines an equipment ownership arrangement where the Managed Services Vendor will own all existing and new printing copying, scanning and faxing imaging infrastructure hardware.
Client organization owned model
defines an equipment ownership arrangement where the Client organization will own all existing and new printing copying, scanning and faxing infrastructure hardware.
Mixed equipment ownership model
defines an equipment ownership arrangement where existing and new printing copying, scanning and faxing infrastructure hardware ownership will vary between Client organizations and Managed Services Vendors.
Fleet management
Services involving the provision of effective and responsive service/support and timely supply/consumable replenishment throughout the duration of the MPS contractual agreement.
Imaging
Encompasses all printing, copying, faxing and scanning activities conducted internally within an Organization on all equipment, whether networked or standalone. Printing and copying services purchased from private sector companies are not included in this requirement.
Imaging Devices
Refers to, but is not limited to, items of print/copy/fax/scan hardware within the following categories: Digital devices, multifunction devices (MFD), printers, copiers, fax machines, plotters, network imaging devices, and scanners. Some the devices may have network connectivity capabilities (e.g. MFDs or network printers), while others may not (e.g. stand alone faxes or older analogue copiers).
Impression
Any image produced (or reproduced) by imaging devices on media including, but not limited to, paper, transparencies, envelopes, and labels. Blank pages located on the back of single-sided prints or copies, or contained in multi-page documents, shall not be considered impressions.
Independent Verification and Validation
Reviews, inspections, and demonstrations executed by an independent entity to ensure quality of service provided by vendors designing, transitioning and managing MPS services. Items may include: verification of findings, review of business case and options, analysis of proposed solution, measurement of quality of service, or determination of actual versus claimed savings.
Manage and improve
Services associated with the measurement of key metrics and determination of overall progress towards the established MPS objectives, and corrective action to be taken as required throughout the duration of the MPS contractual agreement. May also be described as: "Continuous improvement"
Managed Print Solution

A selection of technical and business services used to analyze, transform, manage and monitor imaging environments. The degree of service delivery is based on the individual requirements of each client organization. A full service model may include a wide range of professional services, such as: current imaging environment analysis, optimization recommendations, transition services and management of entire internal day-to-day imaging environment activities (e.g. consumables, equipment maintenance, support, training, equipment ownership, tracking and reporting). A customized service model may consist of only selected MPS services such as: equipment maintenance and provision of supplies.

Within the MPS program, the term "Managed Services" is intended to support internally managed or externally contracted improvement initiatives. May also be referred to as: Managed Print Services

Network Imaging Device
An imaging device attached to the network and available to be accessed by a number of users over the network.
Requirements Definition
A summary of business and technical needs within a selected area in sufficient detail to provide information related to exactly what a solution must provide in order to deliver maximum value. Examples of requirements include: required technical functionality, user needs, performance and quality targets, objectives, costs and transition strategy.
Service Level Agreement
A formal negotiated agreement between (2) two parties the service provider and the service receiver concerning a measurable level of service. For example, it may specify the levels of availability, serviceability, performance, operation or even penalties in the case of violation of the SLA.
Templates
A collection of business tools and documents used to facilitate and standardize the processes of planning, inventory gathering, reporting, cost analysis, transition, solution design and other MPS requirements. Examples include: spreadsheets, planning guides, sample SOWs and reports, and reference information.
Total cost of Ownership (TCO)
A measurement of the complete cost of maintaining (or investing in new) technology based system(s) which includes primary/direct costs such as: equipment, shipping and finance charges, and secondary/indirect costs such as: accessories, software, installation, support, electricity and maintenance/support services acquired over the lifetime of system usage.
Transition and or Implementation
Services which focus on the development and execution of a rollout / deployment schedule to define the steps required to transition the current imaging environment to the optimized state and achieve desired results.
Vendor
The firm, provider, organization, individual or other entity that are qualified and approved to deliver MPS services under the Supply Arrangement (SA).
May also be referred to as: Provider
